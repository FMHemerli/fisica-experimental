Millikan oil drop

Elementary charge from droplet motion

Charged oil droplets rise and fall between capacitor plates. Timing the motion gives each droplet's Stokes radius and charge. Sorting the twenty charges reveals plateaus at near-integer multiples of a common quantum; the mean spacing between plateaus estimates e.

Stefan–Boltzmann

Radiated power versus filament temperature

A tungsten lamp is driven at rising currents. Its resistance (nominal R0 = 0.15886 ± 0.00081 Ω) fixes the filament temperature, while a thermopile reads the emitted radiation. A weighted fit of log U against log T gives the radiation-law exponent.

Photoelectric effect

Planck's constant from the stopping voltage

A grating selects a wavelength from its diffraction angle; each wavelength carries a frequency and a measured stopping voltage. Einstein's relation makes the U0 vs ν slope equal to h/e, so the fitted slope (1.010 ± 0.031)×10-15 V·s yields h. The near-zero work function 0.039 ± 0.019 eV follows from the intercept.
